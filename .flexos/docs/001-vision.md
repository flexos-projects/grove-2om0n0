# Vision & Strategy — Grove

## The One-Line

Grove is an SMS concierge platform that lets boutique hotel guests text a number and have anything handled — powered by AI with human oversight, sold to hotel operators as a white-label service.

## The Problem

Boutique hotel guests chose a 30-room property over a chain because they want personal service. But the concierge desk is only staffed 12 hours a day, the staff are juggling check-ins and phone calls, and by the time the guest walks over to ask for a dinner reservation, the moment has passed. Meanwhile, the guest has a phone in their hand the entire time.

Hotel operators know concierge quality drives reviews, repeat bookings, and word-of-mouth — the three things that keep boutique hotels alive. But they can't afford 24/7 dedicated concierge staff at every property. They're using WhatsApp groups, sticky notes on the front desk, and memory. Requests fall through. Guests notice.

## The Insight

SMS is the only communication channel that requires zero onboarding. No app download, no QR code scanning, no WiFi password needed. Every guest already knows how to text. And unlike a chat widget or app, SMS persists across visits — the hotel can message a returning guest before they arrive.

The real unlock is AI. A well-trained model can handle 70-80% of concierge requests (restaurant recommendations, directions, booking confirmations, local tips) instantly, 24/7. The remaining 20% — the complex, emotional, or unusual requests — get routed to a human who has full context. The guest never knows which is which. The hotel gets 24/7 concierge quality at a fraction of the staffing cost.

## Who It's For

### Primary: Boutique Hotel Operators

The owner or general manager of a 15-80 room independent hotel or small hotel group (2-8 properties). They care deeply about guest experience. They personally pick the lobby furniture. They read every TripAdvisor review. They want technology that meets their taste standard — not enterprise software designed for Hilton.

**Specific moment:** They just got a 3-star review that says "beautiful hotel but couldn't get anyone to help us find a restaurant on Saturday night." They know this is solvable. They just don't have the right tool.

### Secondary: Hotel Staff

Front desk agents, concierge staff, and property managers who use the dashboard daily. They need something faster than their current workflow, not something more complicated. They are not technical. The tool should feel obvious.

### Tertiary: Hotel Guests

Guests never see the product. They only see SMS. They should feel like they're texting the hotel — not a platform. The interaction should feel warmer and faster than walking to the front desk.

### Who This Is NOT For

- Hotel chains with 100+ properties and procurement departments. They need enterprise sales, SOC 2, and integrations with Oracle. Not our fight.
- Hostels, motels, or budget accommodations. The price point and service expectation don't align.
- Vacation rental hosts (Airbnb, VRBO). Different service model, different product.
- Guests who want a self-service app with maps and room controls. That's a different product.

## What It Is

Grove is a B2B SaaS platform for boutique hotels that provides an AI-powered SMS concierge service. The hotel gives guests a phone number — printed on the room key card, the welcome letter, the nightstand card. Guests text that number with anything they need: dinner reservations, late checkout, taxi to the airport, "where can I buy sunscreen?", "the shower isn't working."

Behind the scenes, Grove's AI processes the request. Simple requests (local recommendations, directions, hotel information) get instant, natural-language responses drafted in the hotel's voice. Complex requests (complaints, multi-step bookings, anything emotional) get routed to hotel staff through a clean dashboard where they can respond, delegate, and track.

Every conversation is logged. Guest preferences accumulate across visits. The hotel builds a relationship memory that no individual staff member could maintain. When a guest returns next year, the hotel already knows they prefer a high floor, are allergic to down pillows, and love Italian food.

## What It Is Not

- **Not a property management system.** Grove does not handle reservations, billing, room assignments, or housekeeping schedules. It sits alongside the PMS, not inside it.
- **Not a marketing tool.** No email blasts, no loyalty programs, no promotional SMS campaigns. Grove is for service, not sales.
- **Not a guest-facing app.** There is no app to download. The guest interface is SMS only.
- **Not an AI chatbot.** The AI drafts responses, but humans approve complex ones. The system is designed so the guest never feels like they're talking to a bot.
- **Not for chains.** Enterprise features, bulk pricing, and compliance certifications are out of scope for v1.

## Why Now

Three things converged:

1. **LLM quality** — GPT-4-class models can now generate natural, contextual responses that pass as human concierge communication. Two years ago, this wasn't possible.
2. **SMS API maturity** — Twilio, MessageBird, and similar platforms have made programmable SMS reliable and affordable. Two-way SMS at scale is a solved infrastructure problem.
3. **Post-COVID hospitality reset** — Boutique hotels are booming. Travelers want unique, personal experiences over chain predictability. But staffing is harder than ever. The gap between guest expectations and operational capacity is wider than it's been in a decade.

## What Success Looks Like

### 3 Months Post-Launch

- 10-15 boutique hotels actively using Grove across 2-3 markets
- Average guest response time under 2 minutes (AI-handled requests under 10 seconds)
- 70%+ of requests handled entirely by AI without staff intervention
- Net Promoter Score from hotel operators above 50
- At least 3 hotels citing Grove in guest review responses or marketing

### 12 Months Post-Launch

- 80-120 hotels across US, UK, and EU boutique hotel markets
- Monthly recurring revenue sustaining a team of 6
- PMS integrations with the top 3 boutique hotel systems (Mews, Cloudbeds, Little Hotelier)
- Guest satisfaction scores measurably higher at Grove hotels vs. pre-Grove baseline
- At least one hotel group (5+ properties) on a multi-property plan

## Core Values Embedded in the Product

1. **Invisible technology** — the best technology is the kind the guest never notices. Every UI decision, every AI response, every notification should feel like a human who happens to be very organized.

2. **Taste as a feature** — boutique hotel operators have strong aesthetic opinions. The dashboard, the onboarding, even the invoice should meet a standard of visual quality that makes them think "these people get it."

3. **Staff empowerment, not replacement** — AI handles the routine so humans can focus on the meaningful. The product should make staff feel more capable, not redundant.

4. **Silence over noise** — don't notify unless it matters. Don't prompt unless it's useful. The product should be as quiet as a good hotel lobby.

5. **Memory as service** — remembering a guest's preferences is not a feature. It is the entire point. Every interaction should make the next interaction better.
